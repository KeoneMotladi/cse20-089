History of Kyoto

Originally founded as an imperial capital called Heian-kyo back in the year 794 by Emperor Kammu, Kyoto went through several other leaders or mayors as well as a few name changes.
Kyoto was capital city for over one thousand years, with the name Kyoto literally meaning 'capital city', before having the imperial seat of power moved to Tokyo city in 1868. A few of the names it used to be known as are: Heian-kyo, Miyako as well as Saikyo, which was its name in the Meiji Restoration.

Kyoto became known for its traditional practices such as having an entire district dedicated to geisha; female entertainers who perform various arts, such as dancing, singing and music.

Heritage sites such as shrines and temples also became more and centralized within Kyoto, eventually making the city known for its high precedence and focus on culture
